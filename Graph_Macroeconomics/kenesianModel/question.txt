a. The existence of sticky prices causes the short-run aggregate supply to be horizontal.

In the Keynesian Model, sticky prices and wages mean that firms cannot adjust their prices quickly in response to changes in demand. As a result, the short-run aggregate supply curve is horizontal, indicating that output can change without affecting the price level in the short run.
